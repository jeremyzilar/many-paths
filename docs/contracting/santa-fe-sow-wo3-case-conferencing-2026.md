## Why this work is essential

A shared data system and a by-name list tell providers who is experiencing homelessness and what services they are receiving. But data alone does not coordinate care. Coordination happens when providers sit in a room together, review the people they share, and make explicit commitments about who will do what next.

That meeting is case conferencing. And it does not sustain itself.

Without facilitation, case conferencing meetings happen inconsistently. Attendance drops when the meetings feel unproductive. Providers lose confidence that other organizations are holding up their end. The by-name list becomes a record of the past rather than a tool for active coordination. The coordination habit never forms.

This contract provides the facilitation, structure, meeting documentation, and relationship maintenance that turns a regular meeting into the operational core of Santa Fe's coordinated homelessness response. It is not a planning contract. It is the ongoing work of keeping coordination alive.

The outcomes this work produces are not measured in documents delivered. They are measured in whether providers show up, whether they share information about the people they are working with, whether they leave each meeting with clear commitments, and whether those commitments result in real service connections and better outcomes for individuals experiencing homelessness in Santa Fe.

---

## Background

Case conferencing is the meeting structure at the center of coordinated homelessness responses that work. Communities that have made real progress on chronic homelessness, like those in the Built for Zero initiative, consistently identify regular, well-facilitated case conferencing as the mechanism that turns data into action.

The by-name list gives providers a shared view of who is experiencing homelessness and what they need. Case conferencing is where providers use that view to make decisions and take action together. The two must work in tandem. One without the other produces neither coordination nor results.

---

## City involvement and the path to local leadership

The City of Santa Fe is not a passive funder of this work. City staff are active participants in every case conferencing meeting, and the City plays a direct role in how the program is perceived by providers. When City staff are in the room, providers understand that this is a City-supported effort, not just a contractor running a meeting. That changes the weight of commitments made in the room.

Beyond participation, the City needs to understand how these meetings are designed and run. 59 Projects LLC will not always be the facilitator. The goal of this contract is not to create a permanent dependency on external facilitation. The goal is to establish a meeting structure and a coordination habit that Santa Fe can sustain on its own.

How that happens is not predetermined. In communities around the country that have built successful coordinated homelessness responses, the facilitation role often migrates over time. Sometimes a City staff member with the right skills and relationships grows into the role. Sometimes one of the provider organizations steps up: a coalition coordinator, a well-resourced nonprofit with deep relationships across the service system, or an organization that sees coordinating the group as core to its own mission. In the communities where this work has lasted, that organic leadership usually produces a stronger result than having a contractor run the meeting indefinitely.

59 Projects LLC will be watching for where that leadership is emerging during this contract. Each quarterly outcomes report will include an honest assessment of who in the room has the relationships, credibility, and capacity to grow into a facilitation or co-facilitation role. The City should be part of that conversation, and should be ready to invest in building that capacity when the right person or organization becomes clear.

This does not need to be resolved before the first meeting. The right approach is to start, build trust, watch who steps up, and make the decision with real information rather than in advance.

## Structure of this contract

This contract covers one quarter of case conferencing facilitation, reporting, and relationship maintenance. It is designed to be renewed quarterly. Each renewal is a separate contract; there is no automatic renewal.

At the end of each quarter, 59 Projects LLC will deliver a brief recommendation on whether to renew, based on attendance, engagement quality, and outcomes documented during the quarter. The City decides whether to renew.

Case conferencing does not need to wait for all providers to be onboarded to the data system. The first meeting should happen within the first month of this contract, even if only 2-3 organizations are actively using the by-name list. The meeting rhythm builds trust among providers. The data system supports that trust. Both develop together.

Suggested meeting cadence: bi-weekly or monthly (to be confirmed with providers and the City at kickoff). A quarterly contract covers approximately 6-12 meetings depending on cadence.

## What will be delivered

### Meeting framework

59 Projects LLC will bring a starting framework for case conferencing meetings drawn directly from conversations with cities that have made coordinated homelessness response work. Communities that have sustained this over years have learned, often the hard way, what makes these meetings productive and what causes them to fall apart. That knowledge is built into the structure we start with.

The framework covers the full arc of a case conferencing meeting: how cases are surfaced from the by-name list before the meeting, how the agenda is structured so the most urgent coordination needs get time, how providers present a case without it becoming a long narrative, how commitments are captured so they can be followed up on, and how the meeting closes in a way that leaves people feeling the time was worth it.

This is a starting point, not a prescription. The framework will evolve as Santa Fe's provider community develops its own coordination culture. Some elements will work immediately. Others will need adjustment for the specific mix of organizations, relationships, and client population in Santa Fe. 59 Projects LLC will adapt the framework based on what is actually happening in the room, not apply it rigidly regardless of fit.

### Meeting facilitation

59 Projects LLC will facilitate all case conferencing meetings during the contract period. Facilitation includes:

- Reviewing the by-name list before each meeting and identifying individuals to discuss, prioritizing those with active coordination needs or recent service contacts across multiple organizations
- Preparing a working agenda for each meeting and sharing it with participants in advance
- Leading the meeting through the agenda: reviewing individuals on the list, coordinating on active cases, tracking follow-up actions, and closing with clear commitments
- Keeping meetings focused, time-limited, and respectful of provider staff time. Providers are not attending because it is required. They are attending because it is useful. If it stops being useful, they stop coming.

### Meeting documentation

After each session, 59 Projects LLC will distribute brief meeting notes to all participants. Notes cover:

- Individuals discussed and current status
- Actions committed to by each participating organization
- Follow-up items for the next meeting
- Any new individuals identified for the by-name list

Notes are written to be useful, not comprehensive. They are the record that holds providers accountable to the commitments they made in the room.

### Provider relationship and participation support

Consistent attendance is the most fragile part of any case conferencing program. Providers have full caseloads. Attendance drops when scheduling conflicts arise or when a staff member who was attending leaves the organization.

59 Projects LLC will maintain direct relationships with key contacts at each participating organization between meetings. When attendance drops or an organization has not been heard from, 59 Projects LLC will follow up directly, understand what is getting in the way, and work to address it.

For organizations that are not yet participating, 59 Projects LLC will develop and maintain a light outreach approach: understanding what has kept them from joining and addressing those barriers where possible.

### Coordination outcomes tracking

59 Projects LLC will track coordination outcomes across the quarter:

- Services connected: instances where a provider learned through case conferencing that a client needed a specific service, and that service was provided
- Housing referrals made through the coordination system
- Cases actively coordinated: individuals whose care involved active coordination between two or more organizations during the quarter
- Exits from the by-name list: individuals who achieved stability goals or moved into permanent housing

These outcomes are the evidence that the system is working. They are also the data the City needs to justify continued investment.

### Quarterly outcomes report

At the end of each quarter, 59 Projects LLC will deliver a written outcomes report covering:

- Meetings facilitated (number, attendance by organization, trends)
- Coordination outcomes documented during the quarter
- One or two illustrative examples of coordination that made a difference (de-identified)
- Assessment of what is working and what is not
- An honest assessment of where local facilitation leadership is developing: which City staff or provider organizations are showing the capacity and relationships to eventually take this role on
- Recommendations for the next quarter
- A recommendation on whether to renew this contract, and for how long external facilitation is likely to remain necessary

## What success looks like

This contract is succeeding when:

- Providers show up consistently, not just to the first meeting
- Providers share information about individuals they are actively working with, not just general updates
- Coordination happens in the room: providers leave each meeting knowing what each of them will do next for specific individuals, not just that they share a client
- The by-name list is used as a shared reference during meetings, not just maintained in the background
- At least one concrete coordination outcome is documented each quarter: a service connection made, a housing referral that would not have happened without the meeting, a case where two organizations avoided duplicating effort
- City staff are active, informed participants who understand how the meetings are designed and why

The longer-horizon measure of success is whether Santa Fe develops its own facilitation leadership, in the City, in one of the provider organizations, or both. A case conferencing program that requires external facilitation indefinitely has not fully succeeded. One that has generated enough trust, structure, and local ownership to run without a contractor has.

## Assumptions

- The City will designate a staff member to serve as the primary City contact for case conferencing scheduling and provider introductions.
- The City will make introductions to provider organizations not already in contact with 59 Projects LLC, and will support scheduling where needed.
- Participating provider organizations will designate a key staff contact for case conferencing participation.
- The data platform setup contract is complete (or substantially complete) before the first case conferencing meeting. Providers need access to the by-name list to participate meaningfully.
- Meeting venue or video conferencing arrangements are the City's responsibility. 59 Projects LLC will facilitate in whatever format the City and providers prefer.
