**Version:** Draft, [Date]

## The problem this work addresses

Santa Fe has invested in planning, discovery, and platform development for a coordinated homelessness response. That foundational work is complete. What the City now lacks is the operational infrastructure to act on it: a working shared data system, agreed-upon rules for how that data is governed, providers who are trained and actively using the tools, and a regular coordination meeting structure that makes collaboration a habit rather than an exception.

Without these things, providers continue working in silos. Outreach workers encounter the same individuals week after week without knowing what other organizations are doing. Case managers cannot see whether a client is already receiving services elsewhere. The City has no reliable, real-time picture of who is experiencing homelessness and what progress is being made.

The work described in this program addresses that gap directly. It is implementation work, not planning work. The decisions have been made. This is about putting them into operation.

## What 59 Projects LLC will deliver

This program is structured as four separate contracts. Each is a discrete, bounded scope of work that can be executed and invoiced independently. Together they build a coordinated system from the ground up.

| Contract | Focus | Not to Exceed |
|----------|-------|---------------|
| **Shared Data Platform Setup** | Configure the City's Airtable environment; build provider-facing interfaces; train City staff | $19,500 |
| **Data Governance and Standards** | Establish formal data governance; define data entry standards; produce HMIS field mapping and consent documentation | $19,500 |
| **Provider Engagement and Onboarding** | Onboard initial provider organizations; populate the by-name list with real data; support provider adoption | $19,500 |
| **Case Conferencing Facilitation** | Facilitate regular coordination meetings; track outcomes; renewable quarterly | $19,500 |

Each contract has its own statement of work with deliverables, timeline, and budget.

## Timing

The City has already purchased Airtable enterprise licenses that are sitting unused. Providers are ready to participate. The foundational work is done. Each contract in this program is scoped to produce concrete, documented deliverables within 30-60 days of execution. The four contracts can be executed in sequence or in parallel.

59 Projects LLC is a digital services firm with direct experience implementing coordination technology for homeless services in New Mexico. The firm brings expertise in government digital services, data systems design, and provider training. Prior contracts with the City of Santa Fe (April 2025 and October 2025, under EAD Design Corp) established the foundational strategy, platform, and data model that this program now deploys.
