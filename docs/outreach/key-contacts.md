## Sarita Nair

**Cabinet Secretary, New Mexico Department of Workforce Solutions (NMDWS)**  
[governor.state.nm.us/our-leadership/department-of-workforce-solutions](https://www.governor.state.nm.us/our-leadership/department-of-workforce-solutions/)

**Introduction:** Henri offered to make this connection.

### Background

Sarita Nair is one of the most significant state-level contacts in this space. Her background spans law, government accountability, and urban planning -- and she has a track record of actually moving money into homelessness programs rather than talking about them.

- **Current role:** Cabinet Secretary, NMDWS, since August 2022. Confirmed by the NM Senate 30-0 in February 2023.
- **Previously:** Chief Administrative Officer (CAO) of the City of Albuquerque under Mayor Tim Keller, 2017-2022. First woman to hold this position in Albuquerque's history.
- **Before that:** Chief Governmental Accountability Officer and General Counsel, NM Office of the State Auditor; private practice attorney (governance, public entities).
- **Education:** JD magna cum laude, UNM Law; MS, Community and Regional Planning, UNM; BA, Wesleyan University. Background in international development (UN, NYC Parks).

### Why she matters for this work

- **Henri credits her** with helping secure the funding that brought Urban Alchemy to Santa Fe.
- **She gave Albuquerque $80 million** to address homelessness -- a signal that she funds at scale, not just pilots.
- **She has significant innovation funds** within NMDWS that are relevant to systems-building work.
- As of January 2026, her newly created **Office of Housing** within NMDWS is seeking $45 million in state funds to reduce homelessness, as part of Governor Lujan Grisham's $110 million housing proposal.
- She came up through Albuquerque city government -- she knows Charlie Verploegh's world.

### What to know going in

She is a lawyer and a planner by training -- she responds to evidence, systems thinking, and clear accountability. Not someone who will be impressed by vague vision decks. The by-name list approach, with its emphasis on real data and measurable outcomes, is well-aligned with how she thinks.

**Next step:** Ask Henri to make the introduction. Do not cold-contact.
