### What is CONNECT?

Are you a Santa Fe County or City resident? Do you need assistance with food, housing, utilities or transportation?

Whether you are looking for yourself, or are assisting someone else, the CONNECT Resource Directory can help you find and 'connect' to the support you are seeking by providing a listing of City, County, non-profits, and other providers within the County and City of Santa Fe (and nearby areas). Inclusion in this directory is not an endorsement.

CONNECT is a network of navigators at clinics, community organizations, and city and county programs. Navigators are community health workers, peer support workers, or social workers who link people to services and resources in our community. Agencies in the network are connected through a shared technology platform enabling navigators to send and receive secure electronic referrals, address residents' social needs, and improve individual and community health.

_CONNECT network participating organizations are indicated by an \*._

**To self-refer into CONNECT, please visit:**

- www.santafecountynm.gov/connect
- www.santafenm.gov/connect
- Call 2-1-1 and complete an assistance request

**For more information about CONNECT call:**

- City of Santa Fe Community Services Department — (505) 955-6728
- Santa Fe County Community Services Department — (505) 992-9842

Help us keep this directory current. Contact us to add, revise, or remove information by emailing cmaienza@santafecountynm.gov or calling (505) 992-9842. We encourage organizations to update their information regularly with SHARE New Mexico at ShareNM.org.

## Santa Fe County Community Services Department

The Santa Fe County Community Services Department includes the following three divisions: Health Services, Senior Services and Youth & Family Services.

### Health Services Division

**CONNECT** is a network of clinics, community organizations, and city and county programs who link people to services and resources in our community.

**The Health Care Assistance Program (HCAP)** provides support for health care for individuals who qualify as low income, as well as contractual projects to support the priorities of the Health Action Plan to link individuals by connecting them with the clinical care and community services they need to stay healthy, leading to improved community health.

The Santa Fe County Community Services Department is striving to provide a strong continuum of Behavioral Health care for Santa Fe County residents that can and will positively impact homelessness, overcrowding in the jail, improved public safety, congested emergency rooms, and access to quality health and behavioral health care within our community.

**The DWI Prevention Program** promotes the safety of the people in Santa Fe County by developing and implementing programs to prevent or reduce the incidence of DWI, alcoholism, and alcohol and other substance misuse in Santa Fe County. For more information, contact: (505) 992-9875.

**The law enforcement assisted diversion program, ENGAGE,** diverts or refers individuals suspected of low-level, non-violent crimes rooted in unmet behavioral health needs to high intensity, community-based case management. For more information, contact: (505) 995-6537.

For more information, contact (505) 992-9842 or visit the Health Services Division website: www.santafecountynm.gov/community_services/hhsd

---

### Senior Services Division

The Community Services Senior Services Division promotes and enhances the physical, intellectual and social well-being of seniors living in Santa Fe County while providing many opportunities for creativity, learning and socialization. The Senior Services Division offers a wide range of quality programs and services to residents of Santa Fe County through County operated Senior Centers including congregate meals, home delivered meals and transportation services.

For more information, to schedule transportation or home delivered meals, contact (505) 992-3069 or visit the Senior Services Division website: www.santafecountynm.gov/community-services/seniors

---

### Youth and Family Services Division

The Community Services Youth and Family Services Division includes the Uplift Youth Program, Youth Behavioral Health Case Management, Youth Prevention and Youth Community Violence Interruption Pilot.

**The Uplift Youth Program** empowers youth and families in Santa Fe County through both agency and self-referrals. By fostering strong relationships and developing individualized case plans, the program addresses specific needs and collaborates closely with families to support positive youth development. With more than 30 years of experience partnering with community agencies and schools, we provide comprehensive support to ensure every youth and family receives the resources they deserve.

**Youth Case Management services** provide targeted, youth-centered support for individuals experiencing challenges impacting their mental health, well-being, and life trajectory. These services are structured to address specific needs, promote resilience, and facilitate positive outcomes. For more information, contact: (505) 995-9555 or visit the Youth & Family Services Division website: https://www.santafecountynm.gov/community-services/youthservices

---

### Additional Santa Fe County Departments in CONNECT

**The Santa Fe County Adult Correctional Facility** is committed to creating a safe and secure environment for the residents of Santa Fe County and all those in custody. A Re-entry team was developed in recent years to assist and support inmates when being released from the facility. For more information, contact: (505) 471-4941.

**The Santa Fe County Housing Authority** remains one of the largest landlords in Santa Fe. The Housing Authority currently manages 199 public housing units and 314 Housing Choice Vouchers, through Section 8, Veteran Affairs Supporting Housing (VASH), Mainstream and Foster Youth to Independence (FYI) Voucher programs. The Housing Authority also manages a Housing Choice Voucher Homeownership Program, Family Self-Sufficiency Program (FSS), Resident Opportunity and Self-Sufficiency (ROSS) Program, and Capital Fund Program (CFP). For more information, contact: (505) 992-3060.
