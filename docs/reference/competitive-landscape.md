# Competitive Landscape: Homelessness Services Technology

This document surveys the software and platforms used in homelessness response and coordinated entry, with emphasis on tools that serve **street outreach teams**. It supports grant writing, partner conversations, and product positioning for Many Paths.

---

## Scope

**In scope:** Platforms and tools used by communities to coordinate homeless services, manage by-name lists, track encampments, support outreach workers, and report to HUD. We focus on where outreach teams are supported well, partially, or not at all.

**Out of scope:** General human services or healthcare platforms that do not specialize in homelessness (e.g., generic EHRs, 211-only systems).

---

## How the Space Is Organized

| Category | Purpose | Outreach focus |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| **HMIS** | HUD-required reporting; coordinated entry; system-wide data | Varies by vendor; several now have mobile/outreach modules |
| **Coordination / care platforms** | Cross-agency referral, SDOH, care coordination | Often broad (housing-insecure, not only street homeless) |
| **Outreach-first platforms** | Built for street outreach, encampments, field data | Strong |
| **Methodology / initiatives** | Best practices, by-name list approach; not software | Communities pair with HMIS or other tools |

Many Paths sits between outreach-first tools and lightweight coordination: we focus on **street homelessness**, **by-name list coordination**, and **provider collaboration** in a way that is affordable and tailored for smaller communities (e.g., Santa Fe, then ABQ).

---

## 1. HMIS (Homeless Management Information Systems)

HMIS is the HUD-mandated system for Continuums of Care. It is the system of record for HUD-funded programs, PIT counts, and federal reporting. Outreach is not the primary design focus; several vendors have added outreach and mobile features.

### HMIS in general

- **Role:** Required for HUD-funded homeless services; coordinated entry; system-level reporting.
- **Users:** CoC leads, shelter and housing providers, some outreach programs.
- **Outreach:** Historically weak (desktop, heavy data entry). Newer HMIS products are adding mobile and outreach modules.
- **Public engagement:** Limited; not built for public dashboards or community transparency.

### Open Path HMIS (Green River)

- **What it is:** HUD-compliant HMIS built with "human-centered" design; includes street outreach and encampment tools.
- **Outreach features:** Mobile-friendly; real-time client lookup and data entry; location-based services; encampment management; PIT count tool with geolocation (e.g., "Get Current Location" for encounters). Data flows into a central warehouse for reporting.
- **Audience:** CoCs and agencies that need full HMIS plus better field usability.
- **Differentiator:** HMIS and outreach in one system; mobile-first for outreach; PIT with geographic data.
- **Relevance to Many Paths:** Competitor for communities that want one HMIS + outreach platform. Many Paths does not replace HMIS; we fill the gap for street homelessness and by-name coordination before or alongside HMIS.

### Clarity Human Services (Bit Focus)

- **What it is:** Widely used HMIS with a dedicated outreach module.
- **Outreach features:** Mobile (smartphones/tablets); location-aware; encampment views and assessments; ability to assign and track encampment response teams; GPS and geocoding; assessments and check-ins in the field. Integrates outreach into coordinated entry and HMIS workflow.
- **Audience:** CoCs and providers already on Clarity who want outreach in the same system.
- **Differentiator:** Outreach module built into a major HMIS; single system for HMIS and outreach.
- **Relevance to Many Paths:** Another "HMIS + outreach" option. Many Paths can complement Clarity where the CoC wants a lighter, community-facing layer (e.g., public dashboard, by-name coordination) without replacing HMIS.

### Other HMIS vendors

- **Simtech, Bowman, etc.:** Many HMIS products remain desktop-heavy with limited or no dedicated outreach tools. Communities using them often rely on spreadsheets, paper, or separate tools for outreach and by-name lists.

## 2. Coordination and Care Platforms (Broader Than Homelessness)

These platforms focus on cross-sector coordination, referrals, and social determinants of health. They often serve housing-insecure and at-risk populations, not only people experiencing street homelessness.

### Unite Us

- **What it is:** Coordination platform for social services and healthcare; closed-loop referrals; screening and care coordination across sectors.
- **Focus:** Social determinants of health (SDOH), food insecurity, housing insecurity, healthcare. Used by payers, health systems, and community organizations.
- **Outreach:** Not built for street outreach or encampments. Tends to capture people when they are already in a service or referral flow.
- **Pricing:** For-profit, venture-backed; often expensive for small communities.
- **Relevance to Many Paths:** Many Paths documents the gap: Unite Us does not track people experiencing **street** homelessness who are not yet in that flow. We operate in that gap (e.g., before Life Link, HMIS, or Unite Us).

### CaseWorthy

- **What it is:** Case management and human services software; includes homeless services modules.
- **Focus:** Case management, program management, reporting across human services.
- **Outreach:** Generic case management; not purpose-built for street outreach, encampments, or mobile field work.
- **Model:** Traditional software vendor; less emphasis on public engagement or community transparency.
- **Relevance to Many Paths:** Different niche. Many Paths is outreach- and by-name-list-oriented; CaseWorthy is broad case management.

---

## 3. Outreach-First and Outreach-Strong Platforms

These are built or heavily optimized for street outreach, encampments, and field-based data collection. They are the closest functional comparators to Many Paths for outreach teams.

### Outreach Grid

- **What it is:** Coordinated entry and homelessness data platform; "data engine to end homelessness" with outreach, case management, shelter, and housing tools in one place.
- **Outreach features:**  
  - Outreach Specialist App (iOS); also web on desktop and mobile.  
  - Real-time incident notifications; dispatch-style workflows.  
  - Location tracking for clients and encampments; "when and where" encounters.  
  - Digitized forms and assessments; less paperwork in the field.  
  - Applicant portal for self-reporting; community and enforcement can report sightings.  
  - Chat with self-reporting clients and other network users.  
  - Shelter bed reservation (real-time); housing tools; cross-sector collaboration.
- **PIT:** Digitized PIT count; real-time command center; mobile app with geolocation; HUD reporting.
- **Pricing (as of research):** Tiered per-user monthly (e.g., roughly $169–199/user/month); annual contracts; higher tiers include HIPAA, advanced security, custom reports.
- **Audience:** Communities and CoCs that want one integrated platform for outreach, shelter, housing, and PIT.
- **Relevance to Many Paths:** Closest competitor for "outreach + coordination." Outreach Grid is full-featured and priced for larger deployments. Many Paths aims to be lighter, more affordable, and more focused on by-name coordination and public storytelling for smaller cities.

### Show The Way

- **What it is:** Mobile case management and encampment platform for street outreach.
- **Outreach features:** Geo-tagging; client profiles; ID card scanning; encampment tracking (locations, photos, notes, status e.g. active/inactive). HMIS-compliant data collection in the field. Mobile and web; assistance requests (e.g., for first responders or public).
- **Audience:** Street outreach teams and agencies that need encampment and client tracking with HMIS-aligned data.
- **Relevance to Many Paths:** Another outreach-focused tool. Many Paths differentiates on community transparency, by-name list coordination across providers, and public dashboard, not only field data collection.

### Streetface

- **What it is:** App for people experiencing homelessness to find nearby services (shelters, food, healthcare, etc.) with turn-by-turn navigation and real-time availability. Organizations can list and update services.
- **Outreach:** Consumer-facing (person seeking services), not a provider/outreach worker tool. Complements outreach by helping people find resources.
- **Relevance to Many Paths:** Different role. Many Paths is provider- and system-facing; Streetface is client-facing wayfinding. Could be referenced as a partner or complementary tool.

### Camp2Home / Modesto-style dashboards

- **What it is:** Example (Modesto, CA): Street outreach dashboard using GIS (e.g., ArcGIS Survey123) for real-time visualization, demographics, hotspots, service gaps, and resource deployment.
- **Outreach:** Supports outreach coordination and planning with maps and data; often custom or regional.
- **Relevance to Many Paths:** Shows demand for outreach dashboards and geographic insight. Many Paths’ encampment and dashboard features speak to the same need in a productized way.

## 4. Methodology and Initiatives (Not Software)

These define how communities work (by-name list, quality data, measurement) but do not provide the software. Communities use HMIS, Outreach Grid, custom tools, or platforms like Many Paths to implement the methodology.

### Built for Zero (Community Solutions)

- **What it is:** National initiative and methodology to end homelessness using real-time by-name list data, measurable outcomes, and improvement cycles.
- **Offers:** Quality by-name list guidance; data standards; by-name list aggregator (e.g., upload and metrics); guidance on outreach coverage, provider participation, and population status. Not a full replacement for HMIS.
- **Software:** Communities use their own HMIS or other tools; Built for Zero does not sell a platform.
- **Relevance to Many Paths:** Many Paths can position as the technology that implements Built for Zero-style methods (by-name list, real-time coordination, outreach) for smaller communities.
- **Case studies:** We maintain a curated list of Community Solutions / Built for Zero case studies (by city, county, and region) for reference. See [Community Solutions Case Studies](./community-solutions-case-studies.md).

## 6. Where Many Paths Fits

- **Outreach:** We are building for outreach teams (by-name list, encampments, field use) and will emphasize mobile and field-friendly workflows. We sit alongside Outreach Grid and Show The Way as outreach-aware; we do not replace HMIS.
- **By-name list and coordination:** We focus on the street homelessness gap (between Unite Us and HMIS) and on provider coordination and case conferencing, similar in spirit to Built for Zero but as a dedicated, lightweight tool.
- **Public engagement:** We differentiate with a public dashboard and community-facing story (transparency, accurate counts). Most HMIS and outreach platforms do not prioritize this.
- **Scale and cost:** We aim to be affordable and practical for smaller communities (e.g., Santa Fe, then ABQ), where full HMIS + outreach suites or Outreach Grid may be too heavy or expensive.
- **Lived experience:** We commit to centering voices of people with lived experience in design and governance; we call this out in positioning.
