## 1. Software/Service Overview

**Vendor:** Airtable, Inc.

**Website:** https://www.airtable.com  
**Type:** Cloud-based database and collaboration platform (SaaS)

## 2. Business Need and Purpose

The City of Santa Fe is implementing a By-Name List coordination system for homeless service providers across Santa Fe. This system will enable real-time collaboration between the City and multiple partner organizations to coordinate shelter placements, track service delivery, and improve outcomes for individuals experiencing homelessness.

Airtable provides the secure, HIPAA-compliant database infrastructure necessary to:

- Maintain a shared database of individuals accessing homeless services
- Track shelter bed availability and placements in real-time
- Coordinate case management across multiple service providers
- Generate reports on service utilization and outcomes
- Enable secure data sharing with external partner organizations

This platform is central to our coordinated entry system and will directly support the City's homeless services coordination efforts.

## 3. Compliance and Security Requirements

**HIPAA Compliance:**

- The system will store Personally Identifiable Information (PII) including names, dates of birth, and shelter locations
- **No Protected Health Information (PHI) or medical data will be stored**
- Airtable Enterprise Scale includes HIPAA compliance features
- Business Associate Agreement (BAA) will be executed with Airtable as part of the contract

**Security Features Included:**

- Enterprise-grade access controls and permissions management
- Granular user permissions (role-based access control)
- Audit logs for compliance monitoring
- Data encryption in transit and at rest
- SSO capabilities (if needed in future)
- Admin panel for user management and oversight

## 7. Data Privacy and Governance

**Data Ownership:** All data remains property of the City of Santa Fe

**Data Location:** Airtable hosts data in secure, compliant cloud infrastructure (AWS)

**Access Control:**

- City maintains full administrative control
- External providers have limited, view-specific access through portal interfaces
- Users cannot see data outside their assigned scope
- Automatic user isolation (external users cannot see each other)

**Data Sharing Agreements:** The City will execute data sharing agreements with all external service provider organizations before granting portal access.
