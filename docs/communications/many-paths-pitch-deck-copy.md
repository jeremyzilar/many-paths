## Slide 2: The Problem

**Three gaps that keep us stuck**

1. **Providers work in silos**
   Each organization knows their piece, but no one sees the whole picture.

2. **Community wants to help but doesn't know how**
   Donations pile up where they're not needed. Volunteers don't know where to show up.

3. **The public doesn't see the incredible work happening**
   Without transparency, support erodes and misconceptions grow.

## Slide 3: The Solution

**Many Paths connects what's disconnected**

Three interconnected components that work together:

→ **Provider Coordination**
→ **Community Engagement**
→ **Public Transparency**

*[Visual: Three circles or pillars, connected]*

---

## Slide 4: Pillar 1 — Shared By-Name List + Case Conferencing

**Know everyone. Coordinate care. Close the gaps.**

- A real-time, shared list of everyone experiencing street homelessness
- Providers see who's working with whom — no more silos
- Regular case conferencing to coordinate complex cases
- HIPAA-compliant from the ground up
- Built on the proven methodology of Built for Zero

*"When everyone is known by name, no one falls through the cracks."*

---

## Slide 5: Pillar 2 — Community Resource Coordination

**Connect generosity to real needs**

- Providers post what they actually need — right now
- Community members donate, volunteer, and contribute where it matters
- No more guessing, no more unwanted donations piling up
- A direct line between people who want to help and people who need it

*"Make it easy to do the right thing."*

---

## Slide 6: Pillar 3 — Public Storytelling

**Tell the true story**

- Share the incredible, human-centered work happening every day
- Grounded in rich, real-time data — not anecdotes
- Build public trust through transparency
- Show progress, challenges, and the humanity behind the numbers

*"When communities see the work, they support the work."*

## Design Notes

### Typography
- **Headlines:** Something distinctive with character (Syne, Fraunces, or similar)
- **Body:** Clean and readable (Satoshi, General Sans, or similar)
- Avoid: Inter, Roboto, Arial, system fonts

### Color Palette
Consider warm, New Mexico-inspired tones:
- **Primary:** Deep terracotta or rust (#C44536 or similar)
- **Secondary:** Sage green (#87A878)
- **Accent:** Warm cream or sand (#F5E6D3)
- **Text:** Warm charcoal, not pure black (#2D2A26)

### Imagery
- Abstract geometric patterns or organic shapes
- If using photos: hands, community gatherings, outreach workers in action
- Avoid: Stock photos of homeless people sleeping, sad faces, tents

### Layout
- Generous white space
- One idea per slide
- Large typography — readable from across a room or on a video call
- Left-aligned text, not centered walls of text
