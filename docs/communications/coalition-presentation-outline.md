## Notes for Presentation

**Key Messages to Emphasize:**

1. **We're filling gaps, not replacing your work**
   - You handle affordable housing placement
   - We handle tracking people before they get to you
   - We provide data to help you make better decisions

2. **The housing success rate is critical**
   - This is the main ask
   - Frame as partnership, not criticism
   - Emphasize mutual benefit

3. **The "planting a tree" metaphor**
   - Use this to humanize the work
   - Connects to community building and culture
   - Makes the long-term vision tangible

4. **City partnership legitimizes this**
   - Not just a random project
   - City of Santa Fe is the operator
   - Institutional backing matters

**Potential Questions to Prepare For:**

- "How is this different from HMIS?"
- "What do you mean by 'additional criteria'?"
- "Who pays for this?"
- "What if we don't want to change our process?"
- "How do we know this data is accurate?"
- "What happens to our current referral process?"

**Tone:**
- Collaborative, not confrontational
- Solutions-focused, not blame-focused
- Data-informed, not prescriptive
- Hopeful and realistic, not naive

## Design Notes for Figma Translation

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Peach/coral (warm, approachable) - use your brand colors
- Secondary: Navy or deep blue (trustworthy)
- Accent: Green (growth, success)
- Neutrals: Warm grays

**Typography:**
- Clean sans-serif for readability
- Large, readable type (this will be presented, not read)
- Hierarchy: Big headlines, supporting detail text

**Imagery:**
- Prefer diagrams/illustrations over photos for clarity
- If using photos: Real Santa Fe context, diverse representation
- Icons: Simple, consistent style throughout
- Whitespace: Don't crowd slides, let content breathe

**Accessibility:**
- High contrast text
- Readable at distance
- Color not the only differentiator
- Simple, clear layouts
