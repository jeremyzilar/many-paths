## SLIDE 8

# The Housing Success Challenge

## THE PROBLEM

People are placed in affordable housing  
but get kicked out because they weren't ready.

### Low success rates hurt everyone:
- Individuals experience another failure
- Housing resources are wasted
- Trust in the system erodes
- Providers feel defeated

---

## WHAT WE NEED

Additional criteria for housing placement  
based on real data and readiness indicators.

### Better assessment = Higher success rates:
- People placed when they're actually ready
- Housing placements stick
- Resources used effectively
- Everyone sees progress

# Making It Real: Maria's Journey

### WITH Coordination (20 weeks to housing):

**Week 1:** HUGS meets Maria, adds her to by-name list  
_Needs: Shelter, ID replacement, medical care_

## APPENDIX: Anticipated Questions

**"How is this different from HMIS?"**  
HMIS tracks people once they're in shelters and is for HUD reporting. Many Paths tracks people on the streets before they access shelter systems.

**"What do you mean by 'additional criteria'?"**  
Using by-name list data—how long they've been engaged, services received, stability indicators—to assess readiness for housing placement.

**"Who pays for this?"**  
City of Santa Fe is funding the platform. No cost to Coalition or providers.

**"What if we don't want to change our process?"**  
We're not mandating changes. We're offering data that might help improve success rates. You decide how to use it.

**"How do we know this data is accurate?"**  
Multiple providers updating in real-time. More accurate than any single organization's view. We'll review data quality in monthly check-ins.

**"What happens to our current referral process?"**  
Nothing changes unless you want it to. This adds visibility; it doesn't replace your workflows.
